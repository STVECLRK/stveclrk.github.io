Originally native to West Virginia, I come from a long line of coal miners, truck drivers, and moonshiners. My mother moved our family from the Smokey Mountains to the Naval Port of Norfolk, Virginia when I was 4 years old where I spent my childhood tinkering with electronics, computer code, and mechanical machines.

My first engineering competition was in 2003 at Old Dominion University where I placed first both overall and in 4 separate design and knowledge competitons. I cannot tell you where the award I received is but I still have instructions and materials from my projects from way back then!

Since my childhood, I've spent my life dedicated to learning how things work. From chemistry and electronics to robots and electric vehicles, science and engineering became my life.

Unfortunately, my family wasn't financially capable to support my education and on my 19th birthday, I left for US Air Force Basic Mility Training to earn my college tuition by serving my country.

While in the Air Force, I progressed through many positions, beginning as a dental assistant, and ending as a Continuous Process Improvement Manager for one of the DoD's largest domestic medical centers at Wright Patterson AFB, Ohio.

I served on Active Duty from 2011 until 2021 when I honorably separated as a Technical Sergeant (E-6) to pursue my business and higher education goals, graduating magna cum laude with a BS in International Business Management from American Military University.

After leaving Active Duty, I opened a pool installation company in Dayton, Ohio where I was last stationed to practice the skills I earned during my business degree. At it's peak, my company employed 15 community locals.

In 2022, I was nominated by Warrior Rising, a veteran non-profit, to attend a business pitch competition in Palm Springs, CA to pitch a novel pool construction method I had discovered utilizing insulated concrete form building materials to save building costs and energy loss.

My insulated concrete form concept and pitch won 2nd place in the competition and helped me to highlight blind spots I had missed in my business plan.

In 2023, I decided to put my company on hold and invest my time in developing my product idea. This plan included pursuing an engineering degree at the University of Pittsburgh to develop the skills necessary to improve and eventually certify my own design plans.
